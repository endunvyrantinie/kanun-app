# The EA 1955 Essentials Quiz: Wages, Hours, and Leave

## Overview

This quiz is designed to test and reinforce your understanding of the fundamental provisions of the Employment Act 1955 (EA 1955) in Malaysia, specifically focusing on **Wages**, **Working Hours**, and **Leave Entitlements**. Each question is followed by a detailed explanation, citing relevant sections of the Act, to deepen your knowledge of Malaysian labour law.

## Instructions

Read each question carefully and select the best answer. Refer to the explanations to understand the legal basis for each answer.

---

### Section 1: Wages

**Question 1 (MCQ):** What is the national minimum wage in Malaysia under the Minimum Wages Order 2022?

A. RM1,200
B. RM1,500
C. RM1,800
D. RM2,000

**Answer:** B. RM1,500

**Explanation:** The Minimum Wages Order 2022 set the rate at RM1,500 per month, applying nationally to most employers from 1 May 2022.

---

**Question 2 (MCQ):** For overtime worked on a normal working day, what is the minimum overtime rate under EA 1955?

A. 1.0× ordinary rate of pay
B. 1.5× ordinary rate of pay
C. 2.0× ordinary rate of pay
D. 3.0× ordinary rate of pay

**Answer:** B. 1.5× ordinary rate of pay

**Explanation:** Section 60A(3)(a) requires not less than 1.5× the hourly rate of pay for overtime on normal working days.

---

**Question 3 (True/False):** Wages must be paid no later than the 7th day after the end of the wage period.

A. True
B. False

**Answer:** A. True

**Explanation:** Section 19 requires payment of wages within 7 days after the wage period ends.

---

**Question 4 (MCQ):** On a paid public holiday that an employee actually works, the minimum extra pay (on top of holiday pay) is at:

A. 1× hourly rate
B. 2× hourly rate
C. 3× hourly rate
D. 4× hourly rate

**Answer:** B. 2× hourly rate

**Explanation:** Section 60D(3): an employee who works on a paid public holiday is entitled to two days' wages at the ordinary rate of pay in addition to the holiday pay.

---

### Section 2: Working Hours

**Question 5 (MCQ):** After the 2022 amendments (effective 1 Jan 2023), the maximum normal working hours per week under EA 1955 is:

A. 40 hours
B. 45 hours
C. 48 hours
D. 50 hours

**Answer:** B. 45 hours

**Explanation:** Section 60A was amended to reduce the maximum from 48 to 45 hours per week, effective 1 January 2023.

---

**Question 6 (True/False):** An employer may require an employee to work overtime exceeding 104 hours in any one calendar month without exemption.

A. True
B. False

**Answer:** B. False

**Explanation:** The Employment (Limitation of Overtime Work) Regulations cap overtime at 104 hours per month. Exceeding that requires Ministerial exemption.

---

**Question 7 (MCQ):** How many rest days per week must an employer provide?

A. None required
B. At least one whole day
C. At least two whole days
D. Half a day

**Answer:** B. At least one whole day

**Explanation:** Section 59: at least one whole day in each week as a rest day.

---

### Section 3: Leave Entitlements

**Question 8 (MCQ):** After the 2022 amendments, paid maternity leave under EA 1955 is:

A. 60 consecutive days
B. 90 consecutive days
C. 98 consecutive days
D. 120 consecutive days

**Answer:** C. 98 consecutive days

**Explanation:** Section 37 was amended to raise paid maternity leave from 60 to 98 consecutive days, effective 1 January 2023.

---

**Question 9 (MCQ):** A married male employee is entitled to paternity leave of:

A. 3 consecutive days
B. 5 consecutive days
C. 7 consecutive days
D. 14 consecutive days

**Answer:** C. 7 consecutive days

**Explanation:** New Section 60FA grants 7 consecutive days of paid paternity leave per confinement, up to 5 confinements, subject to qualifying conditions.

---

**Question 10 (MCQ):** An employee with 3 years of service is entitled to paid annual leave of (minimum):

A. 8 days
B. 12 days
C. 16 days
D. 20 days

**Answer:** B. 12 days

**Explanation:** Section 60E: less than 2 years → 8 days; 2 to <5 years → 12 days; 5+ years → 16 days.

---

**Question 11 (True/False):** Paid sick leave entitlement increases with the employee's length of service.

A. True
B. False

**Answer:** A. True

**Explanation:** Section 60F: 14 days (<2 yrs), 18 days (2–<5 yrs), 22 days (5+ yrs), plus up to 60 days hospitalisation per year (totals capped at 60).

---

**Question 12 (MCQ):** A pregnant employee notifies her employer of intended maternity leave, but the employer dismisses her citing 'restructuring' 4 weeks before her due date. The dismissal is most likely:

A. Lawful — restructuring is a valid reason
B. Lawful if 4 weeks notice is paid
C. Unlawful — protection against dismissal during the maternity protection period
D. Lawful if the employee earns above RM4,000

**Answer:** C. Unlawful — protection against dismissal during the maternity protection period

**Explanation:** Section 41A prohibits termination of a female employee during her maternity protection period. Burden is on the employer to prove dismissal is on grounds entirely unrelated to pregnancy.
