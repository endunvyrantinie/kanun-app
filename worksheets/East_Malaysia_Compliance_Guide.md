# East Malaysia Compliance Guide: Sabah & Sarawak Labour Ordinances

## Overview

This guide focuses on the distinct labour laws governing employment in East Malaysia, specifically the **Sabah Labour Ordinance (Cap. 67)** and the **Sarawak Labour Ordinance (Cap. 76)**. While many provisions align with the Employment Act 1955 (EA 1955), there are crucial differences and specific applications that HR professionals and employers operating in Sabah and Sarawak must understand. This quiz highlights these key aspects, with detailed explanations for each answer.

## Instructions

Answer each question to test your knowledge of East Malaysian labour laws. Refer to the explanations for clarity and legal context.

---

### Section 1: Sabah Labour Ordinance (Cap. 67)

**Question 1 (MCQ):** Which statute is the primary labour law in Sabah?

A. Employment Act 1955
B. Sabah Labour Ordinance (Cap. 67)
C. Industrial Relations Act 1967
D. Workers' Minimum Standards of Housing Act 1990

**Answer:** B. Sabah Labour Ordinance (Cap. 67)

**Explanation:** Employment in Sabah is governed by the Sabah Labour Ordinance (Cap. 67), separate from the EA 1955 which applies to Peninsular Malaysia and the Federal Territory of Labuan.

---

**Question 2 (True/False):** The Sabah Labour Ordinance has been amended to expand maternity leave and introduce paternity leave broadly in line with the EA 1955 reforms.

A. True
B. False

**Answer:** A. True

**Explanation:** Sabah's Labour Ordinance has been progressively aligned with the EA 1955 amendments — including expanded maternity leave and the introduction of paternity leave provisions.

---

**Question 3 (MCQ):** A factory in Tawau pays its workers monthly. Wages must be paid:

A. Within 3 days of the wage period
B. Within 7 days of the wage period
C. By the 15th of the following month
D. At any time agreed by the parties

**Answer:** B. Within 7 days of the wage period

**Explanation:** Like EA 1955, the Sabah Labour Ordinance requires payment of wages not later than the 7th day after the wage period ends.

---

**Question 4 (MCQ):** A Sabah-based manufacturer is found running an unregistered worker dormitory. The most directly applicable statute is:

A. EA 1955 — irrelevant in Sabah
B. Workers' Minimum Standards of Housing and Amenities Act 1990 (Act 446), as extended/applied to Sabah
C. Companies Act 2016
D. Customary land law

**Answer:** B. Workers' Minimum Standards of Housing and Amenities Act 1990 (Act 446), as extended/applied to Sabah

**Explanation:** Act 446 was extended to Sabah and Sarawak by amendment, requiring accommodation certification, minimum housing standards, and registration. Non-compliance carries fines per worker.

### Section 2: Sarawak Labour Ordinance (Cap. 76)

**Question 5 (MCQ):** Which statute primarily governs employment in Sarawak?

A. Sabah Labour Ordinance
B. Sarawak Labour Ordinance (Cap. 76)
C. Employment Act 1955
D. Industrial Relations Act 1967

**Answer:** B. Sarawak Labour Ordinance (Cap. 76)

**Explanation:** The Sarawak Labour Ordinance (Cap. 76) is the primary labour statute applicable to most employees in Sarawak.

---

**Question 6 (True/False):** The Sarawak Labour Ordinance, like EA 1955, sets a maximum of 45 hours per week for normal working hours after recent amendments.

A. True
B. False

**Answer:** A. True

**Explanation:** Sarawak's Labour Ordinance has been amended to harmonise key working-hour limits with the EA 1955 reforms — the 45-hour weekly cap being one such change.

---

**Question 7 (MCQ):** A timber-processing employer in Sibu denies paternity leave to a male engineer. Most likely position under current law:

A. Lawful — paternity leave is voluntary
B. Lawful — only EA 1955 has paternity leave
C. Likely unlawful — Sarawak Labour Ordinance now provides paid paternity leave
D. Lawful only if collective agreement is silent

**Answer:** C. Likely unlawful — Sarawak Labour Ordinance now provides paid paternity leave

**Explanation:** Recent amendments aligned the Sarawak Labour Ordinance with EA 1955 maternity/paternity reforms; eligible male employees are now entitled to paid paternity leave subject to qualifying criteria.
